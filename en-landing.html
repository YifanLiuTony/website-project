Why Lithium Fires Are Different

A lithium-ion fire is a chemistry problem, not just a flame problem.

Once a single cell enters thermal runaway, it ejects flammable electrolyte vapor and can propagate to neighboring cells in seconds. Dry powder, CO₂, and gaseous clean agents knock down open flame but do not cool the pack — the fire frequently reignites. Your suppression system needs to absorb heat, penetrate the pack, and keep the adjacent cells below runaway temperature.

1

Rapid cooling, not just smothering

Our lithium-specific foam agent is engineered as a high-stability, micro-fine foam medium with high density and low conductivity. It absorbs heat, blankets the cell, and blocks thermal radiation to adjacent cells.

2

Early detection wins minutes

Image-based flame and smoke detectors sit inside the battery cabinet and use video analytics — flame recognition, linear beam smoke, thermal — to trigger suppression before an open fire ever reaches the hall.

3

Stop reignition after knockdown

Our compressed-air foam (CAF) systems maintain coverage and keep reignition suppressed long after initial knockdown — critical for packs where electrolyte keeps venting after flame is gone.
